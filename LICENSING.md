# KPT 2.3 Licensing Guide

**Author:** Kristjan Jõgi  
**Status:** Explanatory licensing guide

This file explains, in practical terms, how the KPT 2.3 materials may and may not be used.

This guide is explanatory. If there is any conflict between this file and `LICENSE.md`, `LICENSE.md` controls.

## 1. What this repository is

KPT 2.3 is a standard for AI output decision governance.

This repository may include:

- core standard documents;
- trace schema documents;
- basis code registry materials;
- conformance methodology;
- test packs;
- examples;
- diagrams;
- release and explanatory documents.

These materials are public for visibility, review, discussion, and non-commercial use.

They are not released for unrestricted commercial exploitation.

## 2. What you may do without asking

You may, without separate permission:

- read and download the materials;
- share links to the repository;
- quote limited portions with attribution;
- discuss the standard publicly;
- study it privately or in a team;
- use it in research or education;
- evaluate it internally in a non-commercial way;
- adapt it for personal or non-commercial experimental use, if you clearly mark modifications.

## 3. What requires written permission

You must obtain written permission before you:

- use KPT materials in paid consulting or advisory work;
- package KPT into a commercial product, platform, or service;
- build a paid training or certification offer around KPT;
- market a service as KPT-based, KPT-aligned, KPT-certified, or KPT-conformant;
- sell copies, derivative packs, or enterprise implementations;
- include the materials in a paid compliance, governance, or audit offering;
- present your implementation as official, certified, or endorsed.

## 4. Why the license is structured this way

KPT 2.3 is intended to be visible and discussable, but not freely extractable into third-party commercial offerings without permission.

The purpose of the license structure is to allow:

- public review;
- non-commercial use;
- serious technical discussion;

while preserving:

- authorship integrity;
- commercial rights;
- control over official naming and conformance claims.

## 5. Commercial use: broad interpretation

Commercial use should be interpreted broadly.

A use is commercial if it is connected to revenue, paid services, a client engagement, product monetization, lead generation, or enterprise positioning.

Examples of commercial use include:

- a consultancy using KPT documents as part of a paid governance engagement;
- a vendor embedding KPT logic into a paid middleware product;
- a company marketing its platform as KPT-compliant to gain sales advantage;
- a trainer charging for a KPT implementation workshop;
- an advisory firm using KPT as a branded part of an audit package.

If the use supports a business offering, assume permission is required.

## 6. Derivative works

Non-commercial derivatives are allowed if:

- you credit the original author;
- you mark your changes clearly;
- you do not imply official status;
- you do not claim certification, conformance, or endorsement unless authorized.

Commercial derivatives require written permission.

## 7. Can I implement the ideas privately?

Yes, for private study, internal understanding, or non-commercial experimentation.

But that does not automatically grant the right to:

- market the implementation;
- sell the implementation;
- claim official conformance;
- use KPT naming as a trust or certification signal.

## 8. Can I say my system was inspired by KPT?

Yes, descriptive reference is generally acceptable, provided it is truthful and does not imply endorsement, certification, or official status.

Acceptable examples:

- "Inspired by KPT 2.3 concepts."
- "Designed with a decision-state governance approach influenced by KPT."

Not acceptable without permission:

- "Official KPT implementation"
- "KPT certified"
- "KPT conformant"
- "Approved by KPT"
- "Reference implementation of KPT"

See `CONFORMANCE_AND_NAME_USE.md`.

## 9. Can I claim conformance?

Not unless you have separate written authorization or the project later publishes an official conformance program that allows it under defined terms.

Even if your system appears technically aligned, you may not publicly present it as:

- conformant;
- certified;
- official;
- validated;
- approved,

without permission.

## 10. Can I use the KPT name in my product or service?

Not as a brand, badge, certification signal, or official-status marker without permission.

Nominative reference for discussion may be acceptable, but branded or trust-signaling usage is restricted.

## 11. Code vs documentation

Where code is later published separately, it may carry separate terms.

Unless explicitly stated otherwise, do not assume that public visibility means permissive code licensing.

## 12. No automatic open commercialization

Publicly accessible does not mean commercially open.

The intended model is:

- public visibility;
- controlled non-commercial use;
- separate permission for commercial exploitation;
- separate control of conformance and official naming.

## 13. How to request permission

Commercial licensing, official implementation rights, certification rights, or naming permissions require direct written permission from the rights holder.

Until such permission is granted, assume the right is not granted.

## 14. Practical rule of thumb

If your use is any of the following, permission is probably not needed:

- reading;
- sharing;
- learning;
- discussing;
- researching;
- non-commercial experimenting.

If your use is any of the following, permission is required:

- selling;
- consulting;
- training for payment;
- enterprise packaging;
- productization;
- certification claims;
- official-status claims;
- branded marketing use.
