# KPT Origin to Standard Bridge

**Author:** Kristjan Jõgi

**Document date:** 2026-03-11  
**Status:** Informative continuity note

## 1. Purpose

This note explains how the philosophical starting point of the earlier KPT 2.2.2 origin story carries forward into the KPT 2.3 standard without turning the older vocabulary into the normative contract.

## 2. The origin intuition

The KPT 2.2.2 origin story begins from two linked observations:

1. fluent language systems can sound more certain than their evidence warrants; and
2. neither machine nor human participant has direct access to reality itself.

Instead, both operate through mediation: context, framing, memory, symbols, evidence fragments, interpretation, and pressure.

In the origin story this was expressed through the R-M-S-E-A structure:

- `R` - reality exists, but direct access is unavailable;
- `M` - the medium shapes what arrives;
- `S` - symbols, models, and support determine what can be claimed;
- `E` - the encounter matters, including symmetry, meaning, and weaponization risk;
- `A` - action requires a decision boundary.

## 3. What survives in KPT 2.3

KPT 2.3 keeps the same philosophical core, but translates it into a more testable governance form.

### 3.1 No direct access to reality -> epistemic humility
The older epistemological claim now appears in KPT 2.3 as a stricter runtime rule:

- candidate outputs are evaluated as representations, not as self-validating truth;
- unsupported confidence is treated as a governance failure;
- admissibility outranks fluency.

### 3.2 Medium and framing -> zone, context, provenance
The earlier focus on mediation now appears as:

- zone assignment;
- policy context;
- provenance-sensitive evaluation;
- traceable basis codes.

### 3.3 Encounter and symmetry -> weaponization and safety precedence
The earlier concern with pressure, asymmetry, and weaponization now appears as:

- safety precedence;
- weaponized-form handling;
- refusal of disallowed assistance;
- refusal to convert manipulative framing into permitted help.

### 3.4 Action -> decision and enforcement boundary
The earlier action layer now appears in the locked distinctions:

- output != action;
- decision before influence;
- decision before execution;
- decision_state != enforcement_action.

## 4. What changed

KPT 2.3 does not preserve every older term as part of the active contract.
Instead, it freezes a narrower governance core that is easier to implement and test.

The main changes are:

- four decision states instead of earlier broader deliver/qualify/refuse phrasing;
- explicit separation of decision and enforcement;
- canonical schemas and conformance gates;
- trace-first auditability;
- clearer distinction between normative and informative materials.

## 5. Why this bridge matters

Without this bridge, KPT can look like a purely technical runtime framework.
With it, the deeper rationale remains visible:

KPT is strict about uncertainty because it assumes representation-level fallibility.
It does not try to make a language model ontologically special.
It tries to stop a fallible symbol system from bluffing its way into belief or action.
